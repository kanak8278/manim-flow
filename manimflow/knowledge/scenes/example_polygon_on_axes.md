## Summary

Demonstrates always_redraw with a Polygon that represents a rectangle inscribed under a hyperbola y=k/x. A ValueTracker controls the x-position of the top-right corner, and the rectangle redraws every frame to maintain the constraint that its area equals k=25. As the tracker sweeps, the rectangle stretches wide-and-short or tall-and-narrow, always touching the curve. A dot tracks the corner point on the hyperbola. This is the canonical example of a dynamic geometric construction driven by a single parameter.

## Design Decisions

- **Polygon from corner coordinates**: The rectangle is built from four corner coordinates computed from the current ValueTracker value and the constraint y=k/x. The `get_rectangle_corners` helper returns the four corners in the order needed by Polygon.
- **always_redraw rebuilds the entire Polygon**: Rather than trying to move individual vertices, the whole Polygon is recreated each frame. This is simpler and avoids vertex-ordering issues that arise when manipulating Polygon internals.
- **Dot with add_updater for curve tracking**: The dot's updater uses a lambda that calls `ax.c2p(t.get_value(), k / t.get_value())` to stay on the hyperbola. The dot has z_index=10 to render on top of the polygon.
- **Hyperbola plotted with use_smoothing=False**: The 1/x curve uses a very small step (0.01) and no smoothing, which prevents visual artifacts near the asymptote at x=0.

## Composition

- **Axes**: x_range=[0, 10], y_range=[0, 10], x_length=6, y_length=6, no tips
- **Hyperbola**: y=25/x, x_range=[25/10, 10, 0.01], YELLOW_D

## Patterns

### Pattern: always_redraw Polygon Under Curve Constraint

**What**: A Polygon is rebuilt every frame via always_redraw, with its corner positions computed from a ValueTracker and a mathematical constraint (constant area under a hyperbola). A dot tracks the intersection point on the curve. The Polygon's corners are generated by a helper method that converts between data coordinates and screen positions using ax.c2p().
**When to use**: Inscribed rectangles, optimization visualizations (maximize area under curve), geometric constructions that depend on a single parameter, Riemann sum approximation previews, constraint satisfaction demonstrations.

```python
# Source: projects/ragibson_manim-videos/manim_examples/polygon_on_axes.py:13-50
t = ValueTracker(5)
k = 25

graph = ax.plot(lambda x: k / x, color=YELLOW_D, x_range=[k / 10, 10.0, 0.01],
                use_smoothing=False)

def get_rectangle():
    polygon = Polygon(*[
        ax.c2p(*i) for i in self.get_rectangle_corners(
            (0, 0), (t.get_value(), k / t.get_value())
        )
    ])
    polygon.stroke_width = 1
    polygon.set_fill(BLUE, opacity=0.5)
    polygon.set_stroke(YELLOW_B)
    return polygon

polygon = always_redraw(get_rectangle)

dot = Dot()
dot.add_updater(lambda x: x.move_to(ax.c2p(t.get_value(), k / t.get_value())))
dot.set_z_index(10)

self.add(ax, graph, dot)
self.play(Create(polygon))
self.play(t.animate.set_value(10))
self.play(t.animate.set_value(k / 10))
self.play(t.animate.set_value(5))
```

## Scene Flow

1. **Setup** (instant): Axes and hyperbola y=25/x added.
2. **Create polygon** (0-1s): Rectangle appears at t=5 (width=5, height=5).
3. **Sweep right** (1-2s): t goes to 10, rectangle becomes wide and short (10x2.5).
4. **Sweep left** (2-3s): t goes to 2.5, rectangle becomes narrow and tall (2.5x10).
5. **Return** (3-4s): t returns to 5, rectangle back to square-ish shape. Dot tracks on the curve throughout.
